Easy Baked Beef Ribs


Description

These unbelievably tender beef short ribs are a favorite for home cooks who want bold flavors without spending all day in the kitchen. By boiling the ribs first and then roasting them, you achieve a "low and slow" texture in just about an hour of total time.

The secret to this dish is the sweet and tangy sauce made with red wine, soy sauce, and a hint of curry powder. It creates a rich glaze that perfectly coats the meat, making it a simple yet delicious meal that yields three generous servings.

Ingredients

  • 1 (16-oz) package penne pasta
  • ½ cup pasta cooking water, or as needed
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ½ onion, diced or 1 large shallot, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (6-ounce) can tomato paste
  • ½ cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons vodka
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes, or to taste
  • alt and freshly ground pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ½cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
  2. Place ribs in a large pot and fill with enough water to cover. Bring to a boil and cook until meat is no longer pink, about 20 minutes. Drain.
  3. Stir together red wine, olive oil, soy sauce, brown sugar, flour, garlic, and curry powder in a medium bowl.
  4. Coat ribs generously with sauce and arrange on a baking sheet.
  5. Roast in the preheated oven until fork-tender, about 30 minutes. Pour remaining sauce over ribs about halfway through cooking.

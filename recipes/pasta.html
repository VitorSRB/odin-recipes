Gigi Hadid Pasta


Description

Inspired by a famous viral trend, this creamy penne dish is a family-friendly adaptation that balances rich textures with a savory tomato base. By simmering tomato paste with olive oil, garlic, and onions before adding heavy cream, the recipe creates a vibrant, velvety sauce that perfectly coats every piece of pasta.

The addition of a small amount of vodka enhances the flavors while cooking off quickly, leaving behind a sophisticated taste that appeals to both kids and adults. Finished with butter and freshly grated Parmesan cheese, this dish is a quick and comforting meal that can be easily customized with more red pepper flakes for those who prefer an extra kick of heat.

Ingredients

  • 1 (16-oz) package penne pasta
  • ½ cup pasta cooking water, or as needed
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ½ onion, diced or 1 large shallot, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (6-ounce) can tomato paste
  • ½ cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons vodka
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes, or to taste
  • salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add penne and cook according to package instructions until just al dente, about 9 minutes. Scoop out 1/2 cup pasta water and set aside. Drain pasta.
  2. Meanwhile, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat and cook onion until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Stir in tomato paste and cook, stirring, until tomato paste starts to darken and is well combined with the onion, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Pour in heavy cream and vodka and cook, stirring, for 2 minutes until alcohol evaporates. Stir in red pepper flakes and cook, stirring, until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Stir in butter until melted. Add drained pasta and 1/4 cup pasta water. Add more pasta water depending on how thick you like the sauce. Stir in freshly grated Parmesan cheese, taste, and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Continue cooking for 1 to 2 minutes over low heat until well combined.
